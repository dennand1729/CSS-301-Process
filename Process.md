## Upper-Division Electives to Take
All Husky EE students take BEE 332 | Semiconductors & Devices II as a core requirement. After satiating this degree requirement, you should take particular electives to enhance your technical knowledge of integrated circuits. We list some of the recommended courses below:

### Electronic Circuit Design
UW Bothell offers BEE 433 | Electronic Circuit Design as an elective. In this class you gain an understanding of modern solid-state design techniques used for instrumentation purposes. The class focuses on design using integrated circuits (op-amps, processors, etc.) making it an invaluable resource to gaining familiarity with semiconductor technologies which will become products you both sell and work alongside a technical team to implement.

### Fundamentals of IC Technology
UW Bothell also offers BEE 486 | Fundamentals of Integrated Circuit Technology as an upper-elective. This course covers mircoelectronic processing technology, IC-material chemical processes, and teaches design considerations for transistors and other semiconductor components. You will want to take this class if understanding the "why" of component characteristics is on your horizon.
